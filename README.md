# Nifty 50 Sector Performance Analysis — Q1 2026

## Overview

Analyzed Q1 2026 (January 2 – March 30) performance of **12 Nifty sectoral indices** against the Nifty 50 benchmark to identify which sectors outperformed and underperformed during one of the most volatile quarters in recent Indian market history.

## Key Findings

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Nifty 50 Benchmark Return** | -15.18% |
| **Best Sector** | Nifty Metal (-2.48%) |
| **Worst Sector** | Nifty Realty (-27.51%) |
| **Sectors That Beat Benchmark** | 7 out of 12 |
| **Average Sector Return** | -13.07% |

## Why Q1 2026 Was Significant

- Nifty 50 hit an **all-time high of 26,373** on January 2, then fell 15% by March 30
- **US-Iran military tensions** in March pushed crude oil prices sharply higher
- India imports 85% of its oil — higher oil prices weakened the rupee to an all-time low of 94.84/USD
- **AI disruption fears** caused a 24% crash in IT sector stocks
- Foreign Institutional Investors (FIIs) pulled significant capital out of Indian markets

## Analyst Insights

1. **Metal was most resilient (-2.48%)** — Global supply disruptions from US-Iran tensions and continued infrastructure spending supported metal prices

2. **IT crashed hardest after Realty (-24.16%)** — AI disruption fears led investors to sell IT outsourcing stocks; TCS, Infosys, Wipro hit 52-week lows

3. **Realty was the worst performer (-27.51%)** — Cyclical sector hit by rising interest rate fears and rupee weakness making housing loans more expensive

4. **FMCG was defensive but not immune (-14.21%)** — Daily essentials demand stays stable, but even defensive sectors couldn't escape broad selling pressure

5. **Oil & Gas fell despite rising oil prices (-12.58%)** — Indian oil companies are refiners/distributors, not producers. Higher crude = higher input costs = squeezed margins

## Methodology

- **Data Source:** NSE India (nseindia.com) — Historical Index Data & Live Market Indices
- **Period:** January 2, 2026 (first trading day) to March 30, 2026 (last trading day of Q1)
- **Benchmark:** Nifty 50 Index
- **Sectors:** 12 non-overlapping Nifty sectoral indices (NSE publishes 15+ indices, but some overlap — e.g., Nifty Bank is a subset of Nifty Financial Services. This analysis uses distinct indices to avoid double-counting.)
- **Metrics:** Q1 Return %, Distance from 52-Week High, Benchmark Outperformance
